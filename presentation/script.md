# Presentation Script — Data Preparation
**Speaker: Yu Taek Lee**

---

## Section Title Slide
*~15 seconds*

> "I'll cover Data Preparation — the foundation everything else builds on. My job was to take raw datasets and produce clean, leakage-free training and evaluation artifacts for the team."

---

## Slide 1 — Datasets
*~45 seconds*

> "We work with three datasets. **MultiSocial** is an ACL 2025 dataset — 410,000 processed records from real social media posts across five platforms and 22 languages, generated by seven different AI models. It's our main source of training diversity. **HC3** adds 85,000 processed records of human and ChatGPT answers in English."
>
> "Together that's 495,000 records in the training corpus. **RAID** is kept completely separate — it's an adversarial benchmark of 671,000 eval texts, never mixed into training or validation."

---

## Slide 2 — Pipeline Architecture
*~60 seconds*

> "The pipeline runs in five steps. Download, preprocess into a unified schema, embed with Gemini Embedding 2, build a FAISS index, then assemble training files. Each step writes a persistent artifact, so the whole pipeline is resumable."
>
> "The embedding model is Gemini Embedding 2, using the CLASSIFICATION task type. Each of the 495K texts becomes a 768-dimensional vector: 1.4 gigabytes total. The pipeline checkpoints every 10K texts, so it's fully resumable."

---

## Slide 3 — Key Design Decisions
*~75 seconds*

> "Three things I want to highlight. First, schema unification — MultiSocial and HC3 use different column names, so I wrote auto-discovery logic rather than hardcoding mappings."
>
> "Second, leakage prevention. The FAISS index is built on the training split only. When a val or test example queries for RAG neighbors, it can only retrieve training examples — held-out data is never indexed and can never appear as context."
>
> "Third, class imbalance. The raw corpus is 80% AI, 20% human. We produce a balanced 50/50 variant by undersampling, giving 133K records. Both imbalanced and balanced variants are provided."

---

## Slide 4 — What Was Handed Off
*~40 seconds*

> "Here's what was produced for downstream use. The corpus, embeddings, FAISS index, and splits are the shared foundation. The training files come in four variants: with and without RAG context, standard and balanced. The balanced RAG file is a ready-to-train handoff artifact at 221 MB."
>
> "On RAID: pre-computed embeddings were started but the run was stopped partway through. The checkpoint has 450,000 of 671,000 vectors saved and can be resumed if full adversarial evaluation is needed."
